Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Requirements for Hotels

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP) of India establishes strict obligations for organisations that collect and process personal information. As a hotel, you act as a Data Fiduciary and must comply with the Act when handling guests’ personal data.

Rights of Guests

The DPDP Act grants guests several rights over their personal data. Hotels must put in place procedures to honour these rights:

  • Access: Guests can ask for a summary of the personal data the hotel holds about them and how it’s used.
  • Correction & erasure: Guests may request corrections to incorrect information or deletion of data that is no longer required.
  • Withdraw consent: Consent is not permanent; individuals can revoke it at any time. Processing must stop unless another legal basis applies.
  • Grievance redressal: A mechanism must exist for guests to complain about data‑handling practices. If unresolved, the complaint can be escalated to India’s Data Protection Board.
  • Right to be informed: Before collecting data, hotels must explain why it’s needed, how it will be used and who to contact for more information.

Responsibilities of Hotels

To comply with DPDP, hotels should adopt the following practices:

  • Explicit consent: Use clear opt‑in checkboxes for collecting data and make withdrawal just as easy. Parental consent is required when processing children’s data.
  • Data minimisation: Collect only the information necessary for the stated purpose and keep it accurate. Remove unnecessary data according to a retention schedule.
  • Security safeguards: Protect data using encryption, access controls and continuous monitoring. Maintain logs of who accesses guest data.
  • Breach notification: Notify affected guests immediately and report breaches to the Data Protection Board within 72 hours.
  • Retention & erasure: Delete data once its purpose is fulfilled and notify guests before erasure.
  • Transparency: Publish a privacy notice describing data‑handling practices and include contact details for a data protection officer or representative.

Next Steps

Compliance is an ongoing process. Start by mapping the personal data you collect, minimise and secure it, train your staff on privacy best practices and regularly audit your processes. By taking these steps, hotels can build trust with guests and avoid costly penalties.
